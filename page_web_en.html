Marina Poulet


I am an associate professor (maîtresse de conférences) at the Fourier Institute (University Grenoble Alpes).

Research

Research interests

I mainly work on Galois theory of functional equations (difference and differential equations), more precisely :

  • Galois groups of functional equations : Zariski-dense subgroups, computations, applications for transcendence criteria, ...
  • Mahler equations : algorithm (implementation in Python) to recognize regular singularities, radial behaviour of solutions, ...

My work also deals with Tannakian categories and automatic sequences.

PhD thesis

I defended my PhD thesis on 13th December 2021. My thesis, directed by Julien Roques at the Institut Camille Jordan (University Claude Bernard Lyon 1), is about the Galois groups of Mahler equations and the regular singularities of these equations. You can find my manuscript here.
